
The Kiss
1907

The Kiss is the most acclaimed work of Gustav Klimpt of two lifesized figures in a passionate embrace draped in gold.
Considered to represent the apex of his "Golden Period", the painting takes influence from Byzantine works.

Portrait of Fritza Riedler
1906

A painting embellished with gold and silver leaf, the geometric design of this work pulls the viewer away from
her body and the armchair and brings focus to her face. This is accomplished with the semicircular motif lying behind 
her head. The motif is thought of to have been inspired by Egyptian art and the works of Diego Velazquez.

Portrait of Andre Derain

The Portrait of Andre Derain is a work by Henri Matisse that is one of his chief works during what is known as his Fauvist
period. The bright contrast are used to bring out the distinguishing features of the subject.

Le bonheur de vivre 
1905

Le bonheur de vivre, The Joy of Life, is considered one of the most significant paintings in the works of early modernism.
Its bright colors and disregard for normal spation proportions placing a group of nude figures in the center and foreground.

Women in the Garden 
1866

An Early masterpiece of Claude Monet started when he was just 26 years old. His wife and friends posed for the scene 
in a garden Monet rented for the occasion. The dresses themselves were detailed after the initial staging of the scene
based on the contents of a popular fashion magazine.
